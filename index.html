An AI based Advanced Ag-Drone System for Local Farmers in S.C.

1890 Research and Extension

This research proposes an AI based Advanced Ag-Drone system for the Local Farmers in South Carolina. This agrisystem uses ultrasound sensors, a drone (unmanned aerial vehicle), and AI-based Data Analysis techniques for vegetation imaging, automatic irrigation, real-time noninvasive plant health monitoring, and analysis of farm fields while reducing labor and increasing product yield and quality.

Our Work

This project implements AI powered agrisystem that can change the agriculture landscape, the application of drone-based smart farming, precision farming landscape, food security, the future of agriculture, and the challenges ahead.

Leaf Disease and Soil Condition

Faster-RCNN Method

Detect Bacterial Spot disease in bell-pepper plants. Accepted accuracies for the test data with an IoU ratio of 0.5, average precision was 100% and average recall 99.7%. With an IoU ratio of 0.5-0.95, mean Average Precision (mAP) was 99.5%, mean average recall was 99.1%.

Autonomous drone operation

Mavic Air 2

DJI’s Mavic air 2 allowed to fly it in Waypoint mode. In Waypoint mode, we pre-recorded multiple spots in the sky that made up a flight route. When enabled, the drone remembered those spots, and fly those spots autonomously. While flying autonomous mode, we took one extra step that the drone must return to home (where it took off) when it’s finished with the mission programmed it to fly.
